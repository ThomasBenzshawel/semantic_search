The Death of Ivan Ilych, published in 1886, is a novella by Russian author Leo Tolstoy. It tells the story of Ivan Ilyich Golovin, a high-ranking judge in 19th-century Russia. Faced with a terminal illness, Ivan confronts the emptiness of a life lived for societal expectations.
