Fallen Angels, a young adult novel by Walter Dean Myers, was first published in 1988. It tells the story of Richie Perry, an Black American seventeen-year-old from Harlem who enlists in the U.S. Army during the Vietnam War. The story follows Perry and his comrades as they navigate the harsh realities of war and focuses on the camaraderie, fear, and disillusionment experienced by young soldiers in the midst of battle.
