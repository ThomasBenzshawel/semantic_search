This Side of Paradise, F. Scott Fitzgerald's debut novel published in 1920, follows the life of Amory Blaine from his childhood in the Midwest through his years at Princeton University and into his early adulthood in New York City. The story explores Amory's romantic relationships, intellectual pursuits, and quest for self-discovery. The novel explores themes of love, greed, and the disillusionment of the post-World War I generation.
