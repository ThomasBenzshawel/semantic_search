The Chocolate War, published in 1974, is a young adult novel by American author Robert Cormier. The novel follows protagonist Jerry Renault as he navigates the treacherous social landscape of his Catholic high school and defies a corrupt school culture by refusing to participate in a chocolate sale that funds the school’s various clubs and activities. As Jerry faces bullying and resistance, the novel delves into themes of rebellion, institutional coercion, and the toll of nonconformity.
