Ethan Frome, a novel published in 1911 by Edith Wharton, is a tragic love story set in the fictional town of Starkfield, Massachusetts. The story revolves around the eponymous character, Ethan Frome, a struggling farmer trapped in a loveless marriage to his sickly and embittered wife, Zeena. When Zeena’s cousin, Mattie Silver, comes to live with them, a spark of forbidden love ignites between her and Ethan. The novel explores themes of duty, societal expectations, and the consequences of repressed desire.
