The Testaments is a 2019 novel by Margaret Atwood that serves as a sequel to her 1985 dystopian novel The Handmaid’s Tale. Set in the oppressive theocracy of Gilead, it follows the lives of three women–Aunt Lydia, Agnes, and Daisy–whose fates become intricately entwined. The narrative unravels the secrets of Gilead's inner workings and the resistance against its regime. Atwood's compelling storytelling weaves a tale of power, rebellion, and the enduring quest for freedom, offering a chilling and thought-provoking exploration of authoritarianism and resistance. The Testaments was awarded the Booker Prize in 2019.
