Sense and Sensibility, published in 1811, is a novel by Jane Austen that follows the romantic lives of the Dashwood sisters, Elinor and Marianne. After their father dies and leaves their family in financial ruin, the sisters must navigate the complex world of love and marriage. Austen's keen observations and wit illuminate the stark contrast between Elinor's practicality and Marianne's romanticism, offering a timeless exploration of love, reputation, and societal norms in the Regency era.
