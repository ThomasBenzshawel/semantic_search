The Power of One, a novel by Bryce Courtenay, was first published in 1989 and follows the life of Peekay, a young boy growing up in apartheid-era South Africa. Against a backdrop of racial tension, Peekay overcomes personal hardships and discovers his own strength through boxing. As Peekay overcomes personal challenges and stands against oppression, the narrative becomes a testament to the transformative power of determination and the ability of one person to ignite change in the face of adversity. The novel explores themes of racism, resilience, and friendship.
