Ed’s best friend Marv represents the guy voted most likely to succeed. His good looks, lucrative career, and ability to get along with everyone put him on a pedestal. The wreck of a car he drives despite having forty thousand in the bank earns him a reputation for miserliness. No one considers that Marv might have a painful secret that explains his frugality. With Ed’s help, Marv faces the reckoning he’s been avoiding out of fear. Marv reconciles with the woman he loved and meets the child he fathered with her for the first time, bringing joy and peace into his life.
