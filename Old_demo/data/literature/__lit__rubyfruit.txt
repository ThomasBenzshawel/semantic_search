Rubyfruit Jungle is a 1973 novel by Rita Mae Brown that follows the life of Molly Bolt, a spirited and ambitious young woman with a sharp wit and a determination to forge her own path. Growing up in the American South in the mid-20th century, Molly faces societal expectations and challenges as she discovers her identity as a lesbian. Brown’s narrative is a candid exploration of sexuality, independence, and the pursuit of authenticity, breaking new ground in its portrayal of LGBTQ+ themes and female empowerment.
